## Guiding Principles (Keep — worth stating aloud)

These five frame the whole container. Worth naming at the open.

1. **Welcoming everything** — all that arises is allowed.
2. **Assuming nothing** — be careful with assumptions; notice the story and check whether it lands with the other.
3. **Revealing your experience** — he gentle nudge is toward revealing what's truly happening inside, your authentic truth of the moment
4. **Owning your experience** — speak from "I", not blame; recognise your own boundaries and others'.
5. **Honoring self and others** — meet yourself and each other with empathy and curiosity.

## The Toolkit

### Core — In the program

#### The Wink ("I see you")

**How to play:**

- Sit in the circle and simply let your gaze wander around the others — no roles, no rounds, no secrecy.
- Whenever two people land on each other and find eye contact, *both* give a soft wink — a wordless "I see you" — then let the gaze move on.

**Important notes:**

- It's about open, mutual contact, not catching anyone — the wink is a shared gift, given by both at once.
- Runs gently; can sit quietly underneath the arrival/settling beat rather than as a high-energy game.
- Accessibility: a slow blink can replace the wink for anyone who can't wink.

#### Noticing Game (two rounds — right partner, then left)

Run it twice with two different partners, shifting the lens between rounds.

**Round 1 — Noticing (with the partner on your right):**

- Facing each other. Partner A: *"What I notice when I'm with you is…"* (a body sensation, an emotion, or a plain observation — present tense).
- Partner B: *"Hearing that, I notice…"* (their immediate inner response).
- Alternate back and forth for ~4–5 minutes, then switch the lead.

**Round 2 — The Assumption (with the partner on your left):**

- Same rhythm, but now you voice what you *imagine* about the other. Partner A: *"I notice that you seem [calm / guarded / warm / far away]…"*
- Partner B: *"Hearing that, what's actually true for me is…"*
- Alternate for ~4–5 minutes, then switch the lead.

**Important notes:**

- Round 1 grounds in your *own* present experience; Round 2 turns outward and checks an *assumption* against the other's reality — this directly practises "assuming nothing."
- The word *"seem"* is doing the work: it marks your read as a projection, not a fact.
- Stay in the present tense — no past-tense stories, theories, or psychoanalysing. Silence between exchanges is welcome.

#### Five Senses Hunt

**How to play:**

- Silently, each person finds: one thing they enjoy the smell of, one thing they like to touch, one thing that catches their eye, and one sound they can hear.

**Important notes:**

- Works beautifully by the fire (crackle, smoke, warmth, night sounds).
- Can stay fully silent, or invite one share each afterward — your call in the moment.

#### Progressive Somatic Release (+ Shaking)

**How to play:**

- Standing. Begin with **shaking** — shake out hands, arms, legs, the whole body for 30–60s to discharge the day.
- Then tense-and-release muscle groups: squish the toes (like standing in mud) → release; squeeze the hands (crushing lemons) → drop; reach arms high (cat in the sun) → let go; scrunch the face → soften.

**Important notes:**

- This is the front half of the opening grounding sequence — flows into the long-exhale bridge + heart-centered body scan (see "Somatic opening" decision).
- Shaking is your addition and pairs naturally with the tense/release.

#### Monologues

**How to play:**

- One person speaks to a prompt (e.g. *"What brings you joy?"*) for a fixed time (~2 min) while everyone else listens in complete, non-reactive silence — no words, no nods-to-fix, no steering. Then pass / switch.

**Important notes:**

- Honors the talking-stick origin of the circle.
- Keep timing strict (finger countdown 3-2-1) — the structure is what makes people feel safe to drop in.
- Can fuse with body-focused listening (listeners attend to the speaker's body) — strong candidate for the closing main beat.

## Worth Holding in Mind (concepts, not exercises)

- **Transference & projection / "the circle reflects you back."** Resonates with the truth you've touched before — like family constellation: whatever arises in the circle is in some way a reflection of you, and it surfaces by an unconscious rule. Good to recognise and name.
- **Welcome All Responses, Especially Upsets.** A good facilitator model — meet upset with "what's happening for you right now?" rather than fixing. You flagged you have less experience here, so hold it as a learning edge.
- **Circling vs. Authentic Relating distinction.** You find the framing a bit biased — "welcoming everything" already gives the freedom to explore and reveal; and real life rarely affords the full compassion the AR premise assumes. Noted as arguable, not a rule.

## Open Decisions (you asked me to help decide)

### Decision 1 — Main beat ✅ LOCKED: Focus Circle

**Confirmed:** the main beat is a **Focus Circle** (true Circling) — the group turns its shared
attention to one person at a time, reflecting and inquiring with them.

- **Format:** pick **2–3 people** to be circled (3 only if you have the energy).
- **Time:** ~**15 min per person**.

*This is the deeper, more intimate choice (vs. timed Monologues or Free Flow). It asks more of you
as facilitator — hold the time gently, keep the group's attention on the one person, and let
silence breathe.*

### Decision 3 — Somatic opening sequence ✅ LOCKED

**Confirmed — a single flowing sequence, standing → seated:**

```
1. Arrive standing → light SHAKING (30–60s) to shake off the day / discharge
2. PROGRESSIVE RELEASE (tense + release: toes, hands, arms, face)
3. A few slow, LONG-EXHALE breaths (no holds) to bridge from active → still
4. HEART-CENTERED BODY SCAN (~10 min) — the landing; ends with opening the eyes
   → then open directly into the circle
```

This gives one continuous "drop-in" arc: discharge → release → settle → land.

**Note — breath stays soft here.** The heart-centered scan lands the group in an open, surrendered state, so the only breath element is the gentle *long-exhale bridge* in step 3. Keep all breathing soft and natural — no counting or holds, which would re-engage the controlling mind and clash with the surrender.

#### Body-scan script — "A Heart-Centered Body Scan" (~10 min)

*The gesture of care:* before starting, invite everyone to sit or lie comfortably. If it feels right, place one or both hands over the heart or on the belly — a physical reminder of bringing kindness to the practice.

> "Let yourself settle in. Take a moment to just recognize that you are here, taking this time for yourself. You don't need to fix anything right now. You don't need to achieve anything. You are just checking in on a friend — and that friend is your own body.
>
> Let's start down at your feet. Think about how much work your feet do for you every single day — walking, standing, holding you up. Just send a quiet wave of appreciation down to your toes, your soles, your ankles. If they feel tired, let them be tired. Just see if you can say to them, 'It's okay to rest now.'
>
> Moving up into your legs and your knees. Our legs carry the weight of our days. Feel the large muscles in your thighs. If there's any tightness there, you don't have to force it to go away. Just soften around it. Imagine wrapping your legs in a sense of warmth and ease.
>
> Now, bring your awareness to your belly and your pelvic area. This is a place where we store so much of our vulnerability, our nerves, and our history. So often, we try to hold our stomachs in or wish they looked different. For the next few moments, see if you can offer this area total acceptance. Let your belly soften completely. Let it be exactly as it is. Give it permission to expand, to relax, to feel safe.
>
> Moving up to your heart center and your chest. Notice whatever emotional weather is present right now. Are you feeling heavy? Peaceful? Anxious? Tired? Whatever is here, see if you can bow to it gently and say, 'Let me feel what I'm feeling with kindness.' You don't have to push any emotion away. Just hold it with care, the way you would hold a loved one who is having a rough day.
>
> Let your awareness drift to your shoulders and your neck. This is where we carry our burdens, our 'shoulds,' and our responsib ilities. If your shoulders are up near your ears, just gently invite them to drop. Imagine a warm, caring hand resting on your shoulders, allowing them to melt down just a little bit.
>
> Feel your hands. Your hands are so expressive — constantly touching the world, creating, working. Let the fingers curl naturally. Let the palms be completely soft and open.
>
> Finally, bring your attention to your face. Soften the tiny muscles around your eyes. Let your jaw unhinge and loosen. Let your forehead smooth out.
>
> Take a moment to feel your whole body as one complete being — a body that does its absolute best for you every single day. See if you can offer yourself one final, quiet thought of gratitude: 'May I be kind to myself. May I accept myself just as I am.'
>
> Take your time, and whenever you are ready, gently open your eyes."
